My eyes slowly crept open, a soft red light seeping through the curtains of my windows. Rubbing the sleep out of my eyes, I stretch, yawn, and lazily peer about the room.

The air tastes stale, and small dust particles float about the stuffy bedroom. Standing, I shuffle out the door and towards the restroom to start my usual morning routine, piss, brush my teeth, and wash my face.

I finish up brushing my teeth, and splash some cool water on my face, waiting for the refreshing water to relieve the sticky heat that permeates the house.

But I feel nothing.

Confusedly, I splash more water on my face, each time getting more and more concerned. My face is numb, a grainy tingling each time a drop hits my skin, but no sensation otherwise, as if there were a mask of some sort over it.

Beginning to panic, I feel at my face, yearning for any sensation, pressure, pain, anything but this frustratingly numb tingle. I begin to dig into my skin, frantically shoving fingers underneath the surface, diving, searching for a nerve, for anything, anything to get the god damned mess of a mask off.

Chunks of flesh slough off into my hands, slimey and cold, lifeless. The skin piles in the sink, my hands tearing at everything remaining, the open wounds weeping, my hands bloody and my eyes filled with tears, burning the exposed flesh. Finally I begin to feel.

Pain begins to sear through my mind, red static flashing over my sight. In a frenzy I tear more skin, digging for bone now.

Muscle and sinew alike, nothing is safe, everything must go, I must be clean, free of the flesh that confines my face like a prison, suffocating me, choking me.

Clean.
I must be clean.
I must feel something, anything at all, anything but this numbness.

Finally, the scrapping of nail and bone, fiery pain scorching what nerves are left, exposed blood vessels and tissue dangling from my now free and unladen face.

Finally, I can feel.
